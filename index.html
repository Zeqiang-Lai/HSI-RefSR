Abstract


Fusion-based hyperspectral image (HSI) super res- olution has become even more prevalent recently for its ability to integrate high-frequency information from the paired high- resolution RGB image. However, most of the existing methods either heavily rely on the accurate alignment between HSI and RGB image, or can only deal with simulated unaligned RGB guidance generated by rigid geometric transformation, which weakens their effectiveness for real scenes. In this paper, we propose a new architecture for the fusion-based HSI super- resolution using real unaligned RGB reference with both rigid and non-rigid transformations. In order to achieve this, our network generalizes the traditional encoder-decoder structure with an extra pixel-wise alignment module to explicitly align the multi-level deep features of RGB reference with HSI using estimated optical flow. The aligned features are further adjusted by an adaptive attention module to focus more on well-aligned and informative regions before sending them to the fusion decoder to produce the final high-resolution output. To evaluate the proposed model for real scenes, we additionally collect a real- world HSI fusion dataset, consisting of paired HSI and unaligned RGB reference. Extensive experiments on simulated and our real dataset demonstrate a clear improvement of our method over existing single-image and fusion-based methods on quantitative assessment and visual comparison.
